## Definitions and strategy

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion>
<AccordionItem title="What is IBM Design?">

IBM Design is all of the design offices, organizations and teams at IBM coming together into one unified practice.

</AccordionItem>

<AccordionItem title="What is IBM Design Language?">

IBM Design Language is the new, unified, open-source design system for IBM. It covers the philosophy, principles and practices that govern and guide every experience delivered by IBM. When IBM Design Language has been successfully rolled out, around the world and across the corporation, all IBM brand experiences will be unified, instantly identifiable as IBM and differentiated in the marketplace.

</AccordionItem>

<AccordionItem title="What is IBM Product Design (aka Carbon)?">

IBM Product Design (Carbon) is a discipline within IBM Design. Carbon is the open-source design system for all IBM software products. It is a series of individual styles, components, and guidelines used for creating unified UI.

</AccordionItem>

<AccordionItem title="What is IBM Digital Design?">
IBM Digital Design is a discipline within IBM, which includes a series of individual styles, components, and guidelines used for creating a unified look and feel across all marketing and editorial experiences on ibm.com. Included in this system are elements, components, guidelines, a design kit, code examples and instructions on how to use the v19a codebase.
</AccordionItem>

<AccordionItem title="What is IBM Design Research?">
IBM Design Research is a discipline within IBM, that drives actionable user insights. The research guides build continuous knowledge, discovery, and empathy. They develop understanding through empirical observation, experience, and creation.
</AccordionItem>

<AccordionItem title="How does the IBM Design Language site relate to the IBM Brand Center? ">
The content currently available within the IBM Brand Center will soon be accessible via a new section called Brand Design Disciplines, using the switcher at the top right of the IBM Design Language site. This switcher enables users to easily move between the IBM Design properties and disciplines sites. The left navigation section that is visible will be local to the site selected via the switcher.
</AccordionItem>

<AccordionItem title="What happened to Duo?">
“Duo” was the code name formerly used for IBM Design Language., new IBM Design Language.
</AccordionItem>

<AccordionItem title="What is the business driver behind the creation of IBM Design Language? ">IBM Design Language was created as a result of the design teams across IBM uniting as one to create one common system. The IBM Design Language describes how to express this system, in actionable guidance.  The Design philosophy that forms the backbone of the IBM Design Language is in line with other efforts within IBM that encourage building upon existing work, valuing quality over quantity, using expert curation as a form of expression and creating thoughtful, crafted content.
</AccordionItem>

</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>


##  Guidance and contributions

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion>

<AccordionItem title="Can I contribute to IBM Design Language, or the disciplines, for example, Product Design, Digital Design, Event and so on?">

Yes, please visit the Contributing page for general information. We have specific dedicated guidelines for developers and designers alike. We appreciate your interest in helping us build the IBM Design Language. We are currently developing specific guidelines for developers and designers alike. These will be available on the Contributing page.

</AccordionItem>

<AccordionItem title="When should we start using new IBM Design Language guidance?">

Please start using the guidance right away. All new IBM materials need to incorporate the IBM Design Language. If you are working on a design element for a legacy product, you can access the [v1 Language site](https://www.ibm.com/design/v1/language/) or via the blue bar at the top of the IBM Design Language site.

</AccordionItem>

<AccordionItem title="How can my agency access the IBM Design Language guidance?">

Approved IBM agency partners will be able to access the IBM Design Language site via the external URL: [ibm.com/design/language](https://www.ibm.com/design/language/). If your agency does not yet have access to any sections of the site that they need, please follow the instructions listed [here](https://w3.ibm.com/design/language/partner-access/).

</AccordionItem>

<AccordionItem title="Why can I/my agency still access the old guidance at ibm.com/design/v1/language?">

IBM needs to continue to support the v1 Design Language site because designers are still working on products and websites that use the previous version of the guidance. Legacy products and materials will continue to exist for quite some time.

</AccordionItem>

<AccordionItem title="Will my agency be able to attend education on the IBM Design Language? ">

Yes. IBM-approved agency partners will be invited to attend a briefing on IBM Design Language. Please check back here soon.

</AccordionItem>

<AccordionItem title="When will more materials be available, for example to help us create new marketing collateral, create materials for an upcoming event, or redesign a local Brand Center?">

All guidance available for use now is on the IBM Design Language website. New sections are continuously added (please refer to the release schedule on the [Updates]() page.)

</AccordionItem>

<AccordionItem title="Does the IBM Brand Experience Design team need to see new materials designed at IBM?">

The IBM Brand Experience Design team would like the opportunity to provide input on all new materials designed at IBM, whether they are designed for internal or external use. Please let the team know of new design projects and briefs as early as possible via the IBM Slack Channel [#ibm-design-language](https://ibm-studios.slack.com/messages/C0U35LB2B), so that they can provide input. Please include a brief description of the project, the timeline and the names of the main IBM and agency contacts. If you are an agency partner, or do not have access to IBM’s Slack channels, please email these details to: [designlanguage@au1.ibm.com]().

</AccordionItem>

<AccordionItem title="What is the approval process for new design work now?">

The first step in the approval process is to ensure a member of the Brand Experience Design team is looped in for all new work, to ensure it is aligned to the IBM Design Language. To advise the team of new projects and coordinate their participation, please send a message to the Slack channel [#ibm-design-language](https://ibm-studios.slack.com/messages/C0U35LB2B), with a brief description of the project, timeline and the names of the main contacts. The approval process will be formalized for your project once the Brand Experience Design team has been looped in. If you are an agency partner, or do not have access to IBM’s Slack channels, please email these details to: [designlanguage@au1.ibm.com]().

</AccordionItem>

<AccordionItem title="Are we still using the “Looks like, Sounds like, Thinks like, Performs like” model for authoring and authorizing content designed at IBM, or has this been superseded by IBM Design Language?">

IBM Design Language is in line with the “Looks like, Sounds like, Thinks like, Performs like” model. Please continue to use that model, along with the IBM Design Language, to develop high-quality design experiences.

</AccordionItem>

</AccordionItem>
